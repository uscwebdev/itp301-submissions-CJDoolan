Design Portfolio Website

As an aspiring product designer, I found it incredibly rewarding to code my design portfolio website. It allowed me to showcase my skills and creativity in both design and development. I started by sketching my ideas and creating a Figma wireframe. Then, I translated those ideas into code using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It was a challenge to make the site visually appealing and user-friendly. I enjoyed experimenting with different layouts, typography, and color schemes until I found the perfect combination. Overall, coding my design portfolio was a great experience that allowed me to combine my passion for design and development.

Website: Psychology of Design

The goal of this website was to show users how they might construct their site in a way that was both visually appealing and understandable. The 5 main topics I focused on were Shape, Color, Mental Models, Gestalt Principles, and Dual Processing. I wanted to combine psychology principles and design principles into an interactive and educational page.
